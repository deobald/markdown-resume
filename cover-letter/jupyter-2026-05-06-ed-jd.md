Executive Director, Jupyter Foundation (Part-time Contractor role)

The Jupyter Foundation is entering a critical phase of growth. Approximately one year into our formation, we are seeking an Executive Director to define and operationalize our value proposition, and solidify and grow our relationships with member organizations.

This is currently a part-time executive role focused on high-impact member engagement. Your primary mandate is to ensure that our Premier and General members clearly understand, realize, and can internally articulate the value of their foundation membership. You will act as a key partner to member organizations and their representatives, facilitating communication back and forth with the community to ensure members derive value from the relationship, while respecting the technical independence of the project.

Note: As we refine the operational needs of the Foundation, this role has the strong potential to evolve into a full-time position encompassing broader community leadership and ecosystem expansion.

Key Responsibilities

    Member Success & Value Realization (Primary Focus) - Retention & Renewal: Own the renewal strategy for existing members. Proactively engage with member representatives to ensure they are getting what they need from the Foundation well before contract renewal dates. - Internal Advocacy Support: Equip member representatives with the data, narratives, and strategic insights they need to justify continued investment in Jupyter to their internal stakeholders and budget holders. - Value Mapping: Work with Premier and Gneral members to map the Foundation’s activities to their specific business goals, ensuring the "ROI" of open source sponsorship is tangible and visible. - Creating Participation Mechanisms: Work within, design, and improve the structures that provide members with the agency they desire and deliver the specific value they expect from their membership (such as working groups, roundtables, or strategic forums). - Onboarding: Professionalize the onboarding process for new members, setting clear expectations and establishing communication channels immediately upon signing.

    Strategic Growth & Business Development - Pipeline Partnership: Collaborate closely with the Linux Foundation Member Development team to identify and close new memberships. While the LF team handles the logistics, you will provide the domain expertise and the "closer" narrative. You will own the Sales strategy for which stakeholders are our best bet for success. - Value Proposition Refinement: Continuously iterate on the Jupyter Foundation pitch. Distinguish the unique value props for different industries (Tech, Finance, Academia) and different tiers (Premier vs. General). - Ecosystem Networking: Represent the Foundation at key industry events (e.g., JupyterCon, PyData) to cultivate relationships with potential high-value stakeholders.

    Foundation Operations & Governance - Board Leadership: Collaboratively drive Governing Board and subcommittee meetings alongside the Board Chair, Administrative team and other meeting organizers, including agenda setting, strategy facilitation, and action item tracking and delivery. You will be the accountable party for many of the actions that come out of meetings. - Fiscal Stewardship: Own the creation and management of the yearly budget in collaboration with the Board and Finance committee, ensuring resources are allocated to high-impact initiatives. - LF Liaison: Serve as the primary point of contact for Linux Foundation shared services (Legal, Finance, Marketing, Events), ensuring the Foundation leverages the full suite of available support.

    Stakeholder Representation & Facilitation - Facilitating Communication: Act as the primary conduit for information between the Foundation (commercial members) and the wider Jupyter community. You will ensure member interests and feedback are effectively communicated to the community, and conversely, that community innovations and needs are translated into value for members. - Thought Leadership: Help summarize and broadcast the impact of Project Jupyter to external audiences, elevating the visibility of the project and its sponsors.

Preferred Qualifications - Domain Knowledge: Familiarity with the Data Science, AI/ML, or Interactive Computing landscape (Python, Jupyter, PyData ecosystem). - Non-Profit/Foundation Experience: Previous experience working with 501(c)(6) trade associations or open source foundations.

Salary: $150,000 USD
